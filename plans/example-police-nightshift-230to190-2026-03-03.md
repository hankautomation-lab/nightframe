### 1. Quick Summary

You’ve got weird hours, long nights, and a knee that’s seen some shit. We’ll respect that and still move you forward.

### 2. Weekly Workout Plan

You train **3 days/week**, all **full‑body**, each with one heavy compound focus that is **knee‑friendly**. We’ll avoid deep knee‑dominant moves that piss off your ACL and lean more on hinges + controlled leg work.

You can plug these three days into whatever days line up with energy/schedule (e.g., Mon/Wed/Sat). Focus on structure, not exact weekday names.

#### Day A – Heavy Hinge / Posterior Chain Focus (ACL‑friendly)

**Primary (heavy):**

- Trap‑bar deadlift **or** Romanian deadlift  
  - 3–4 sets × 4–6 reps  
  - Rest 2–3 min between sets  
  - Focus on hip hinge, not knee bend.

**Accessories:**

- **Push:**  
  - Flat or incline dumbbell bench press – 3×8–10  
- **Pull:**  
  - Chest‑supported row or cable row – 3×8–12  
- **Legs (knee‑friendly):**  
  - Leg curl machine or glute‑ham raises – 3×10–12  
- **Core:**  
  - Planks – 3×30–45s

**Notes:**  
This is your main “strength” day. You’ll feel it in hamstrings/glutes/back, not in the front of your knee.

---

#### Day B – Heavy Upper Push Focus (Bench Day)

**Primary (heavy):**

- Barbell bench press or Smith‑machine bench press  
  - 3–5 sets × 4–6 reps

**Accessories:**

- **Legs (controlled):**  
  - Leg press with **moderate ROM**, feet higher on the platform – 3×8–12  
    - No ego weights, no deep knee flexion. If knee feels sketchy, cut ROM or drop weight.  
- **Pull:**  
  - Lat pulldown or assisted pull‑ups – 3×6–10  
- **Shoulders:**  
  - Seated dumbbell shoulder press – 3×8–10  
- **Core:**  
  - Farmer’s carries – 4×20–30m

**Notes:**  
This day balances upper pressing strength with safe leg work that doesn’t demand heavy knee stability in unstable positions.

---

#### Day C – Posterior + Row Focus (Back + Glutes)

**Primary (heavy):**

- Barbell row **or** chest‑supported row (if back tired)  
  - 3–4 sets × 6–8 reps

**Accessories:**

- **Legs (glute emphasis, ACL‑friendly):**  
  - Hip thrusts or glute bridges – 3×8–12  
- **Single‑leg work, controlled:**  
  - Step‑ups to a low/medium box or supported split squats – 3×8–10 per leg  
    - Only if knee tolerates it; hold on to something, control the descent.  
- **Push:**  
  - Push‑ups or machine press – 3×10–15  
- **Core:**  
  - Dead bugs or cable anti‑rotation press – 3×10–12

**Notes:**  
This is your “posture and power” day — back, glutes, and controlled knee-friendly single‑leg work.

### 3. Cardio Plan

You chose **no running**, experience level **intermediate** → incline treadmill walking as your main tool.

With 3 lifting days/week, cardio days = **round_up(3 × 0.66) ≈ 2–3 days**.

#### Cardio Day 1 – Moderate Incline Walk

- Treadmill:
  - Incline: 10–12%  
  - Speed: 3.0–3.3 mph  
  - Duration: 30–35 minutes

Keep this on a **non‑leg‑heavy day** if possible (or later in the day if you lifted earlier).

---

#### Cardio Day 2 – Slightly Harder Incline

- Treadmill:
  - Incline: 12–15%  
  - Speed: 3.0–3.5 mph  
  - Duration: 25–30 minutes

RPE should be 7–8/10 by the end — breathing up but not dying.

---

#### Optional Cardio Day 3 – Shorter but Steeper

Only add this if your week isn’t crushing you.

- Treadmill:
  - Incline: 12–15%  
  - Speed: 3.0–3.3 mph  
  - Duration: 20–25 minutes

Put this on a day **away** from your heaviest lower‑body session (Day A).

### 4. Nutrition Numbers

Target weight: **190 lbs**

- **Calories:**  
  - 190 × 10 = **1,900 kcal/day**

- **Protein:**  
  - 190 + 10 = **~200 g protein/day**

How to use this:

- Aim for **~200 g protein** every day. If you’re short, a shake or two makes up the difference.
- After protein (~800 kcal), you’ve got **~1,100 kcal** left for carbs and fats.
- On **shift days**:
  - Keep meals simple and repeatable: 1–2 anchor meals + 1–2 snacks that don’t wreck your stomach on duty.
- On **days off / heavier training days**:
  - Bias a bit more carbs (rice, potatoes, oats) around training as long as you respect the same total.

This isn’t a “never eat X” plan. It’s: hit protein, roughly respect calories, and let the rest flex around your shifts.

### 5. Injury / Constraint Notes

- Reported issue: **Right ACL tear 3 years ago**.
- Adjustments made:
  - Emphasis on **hinge‑dominant** leg work (deadlifts, RDLs, hip thrusts) over deep squats/lunges.
  - Squat patterns replaced with:
    - Leg press with controlled ROM and higher foot placement.
    - Low‑box step‑ups or supported split squats only if pain‑free.
  - No mandatory barbell back squats; if you do them at all, they’re optional and conservative.

General rules:

- Any movement that gives sharp pain in that knee → **swap it immediately**, don’t grind through.
- Preferred swaps:
  - Back squat → goblet squat to a box, leg press, or not at all.
  - Deep lunges → smaller ROM split squats or just skip and lean more on hinges.
- If swelling or instability shows up, back off lower‑body load and talk to a real human pro (physio/ortho).

This is guidance, not medical clearance.

### 6. Chad’s Note

You’re doing a job where you don’t get to screw up. Between nights, weird start times, and an old ACL, it’d be very easy to say “I’ll fix this later” and just keep sliding.

This plan isn’t about turning you into a fitness freak. It’s about:

- Keeping your legs and back strong enough to handle the gear, the shifts, and the years.  
- Letting you lean down without needing a second job just to manage your training.  
- Giving you clear boxes to tick so you don’t have to make a thousand tired decisions every week.

Hit your 3 lifts. Hit 2–3 incline walks. Aim for ~1,900 kcal and ~200 g protein most days.  
If a week goes sideways — calls, overtime, life — you **don’t quit**. You do what you can, then you come back to this frame the next week.

You’re already carrying enough weight in your job. We’re just trimming the kind you don’t need.
